# Cover Letter — Senior Technical Game Designer, Eleventh Hour Games

---

Dear Eleventh Hour Games Team,

I design systems for a living, and I play games the same way — always one layer deeper than the surface. Most of that literacy came from thousands of hours in Maplestory and Warframe: games where progression architecture, build identity, farming loops, and long-term player retention are the whole product. That's where I learned to think about how itemization creates agency, how grinding can feel rewarding or hollow, and what makes players invest hundreds of hours chasing incremental power. Working on a loot-driven action RPG like Last Epoch is a direct extension of the design problems I find most interesting.

I'm Dekel Riess, a Game Design & Development student at Tiltan College of Arts (graduating 2028), and I've been building games hands-on since day one of the program. My work spans multiple genres and engines, with a focus on systems design and technical implementation:

**Android Survival Game (Unity)** — I designed and implemented a full meta progression system from the ground up: resource economies, unlock trees, persistent upgrade loops that layer over the core survival gameplay. This project pushed me to think about long-term player engagement the same way you have to in an ARPG — how do you keep the loop feeling rewarding fifty hours in?

**Dice Bound (Unity)** — An idle RPG with dice-based combat and layered progression. I own the full design and implementation, including stat systems, enemy scaling, and the interaction model between dice outcomes and character builds. It's a smaller scope, but it's where I've done the most intentional design work on interconnected systems.

**Balatro-inspired card game (C#)** — Built a roguelike card game from scratch in C#, handling all of the game logic, state management, and card interaction systems. This sharpened my ability to implement complex, edge-case-heavy rule systems cleanly.

I know I'm earlier in my career than this role typically calls for, and I'm not going to pretend otherwise. What I can tell you is that I think about systems design with a level of depth that I believe is rare for where I am — and that ARPG design specifically is something I've studied seriously, both as a player and as a designer.

I'd love the chance to show you more of my work and talk about how I think about these problems. My portfolio is at [your portfolio URL here].

Thank you for building Last Epoch, and for reading this.
